The 4-Stage ICT Adoption Framework

For factory owners, the primary risk is Production Interruption. Our roadmap prioritizes non-invasive integration.

Stage 1: Asset Auditing & Instrumentation

Identify "Dark Assets"—machines that provide no data. Install external IoT vibration and thermal sensors to establish a baseline without touching the machine’s internal logic.

Stage 2: Connectivity & Edge Computing

Deploy Edge Gateways to filter noise. Only relevant anomalies are sent to the Cloud, reducing bandwidth costs by up to 60%.

Stage 3: Systems Integration (ERP/MES)

Connect the shop floor to the office. Real-time production counts automatically update inventory levels and financial forecasts.

Stage 4: Autonomous Optimization

Apply AI to adjust machine parameters automatically for energy efficiency and waste reduction.

Common Pitfalls to Avoid

  • The "Flashy Tech" Trap: Buying AI tools before you have clean, structured data.
  • Ignoring the Human Factor: Failing to train floor operators on new digital interfaces leads to "shadow systems" (paper logs).
  • Security Afterthought: Connecting legacy PLCs to the internet without a DMZ or firewall.
